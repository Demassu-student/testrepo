Usability Study: findings
I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.
Round 1 findings
  1. Users need search screen, mostly they tend to find specific product
  2. Users want filter options, to sort for their specific needs
  3. Users feel unsure about calories estimation information
Round 2 findings
  1. At the first glance, Users don‚Äôt expect nutrition tracking in a cat food delivery service.
  2. Users mostly use the same flow (easy path) even when they were asked using different paths (indirect goals) and usually want to recurrent order
  3. Users demand more screen and clickable component to interact

Accessibility considerations
I created the sitemap to organize the screen and what kind of information can be put on the screen.
  1. Text with crucial information used colors that pass accessibility contrast WCAG minimum 4.5:1 AA
  2. For legibility, there is not a text-in-used smaller than 10px
  3. Clear navigation & information system. User can easily go back and reverse the step (in checkout screen)

Impact
While designing the Nutricats app, I learned that the assumption or ideas are only the beginning of the process. The ideas can be overwhelmed the user, thus only prioritizing what the user needs not what the user might want. Also, present information in an easily digested manner. Usability studies and peer feedback really shaped each iteration of the app‚Äôs designs.
Next Steps
  1. Iterate the need to improve the interaction and add more clickable screen paths, especially on the product screen, search screen & receipt screen because these screens are most likely to get a lot of visitors.
  2. Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.
  3. Conduct more user research on the real users from similar apps or competitors to determine any new areas of need.
